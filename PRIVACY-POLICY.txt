1. WHAT THIS POLICY IS BASED ON
   The policy below was written to match what the NexoGreen EXIM website
   ACTUALLY does today, not a generic template. Specifically:

   - DATA COLLECTED: Two forms only.
       • Contact form  -> name, company, corporate email, phone, country,
         message/description.
       • Request-a-Quote (RFQ) form -> name, company, corporate email, phone,
         destination port/country, product category, volume, unit, target
         price, packaging notes, message.
     Both are B2B business-contact details. The site does not ask for, and you
     should not collect, sensitive personal data (financial account numbers,
     government IDs, health, biometrics, etc.) through these forms.

   - FORM PROCESSING: A third-party form service (Formspree / Web3Forms) is the
     intended processor. In js/forms.js the endpoint is still the placeholder
     "YOUR_FORMSPREE_ENDPOINT" and no live submission happens yet (Phase 1
     confirms receipt in the browser only). The policy reflects that a
     third-party form processor is/will be used.

   - THIRD PARTIES THAT RECEIVE DATA:
       • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com) — loaded on
         every page; Google receives the visitor's IP address when fonts load.
       • The form service (above).
       • Your static web host — keeps standard server access logs (IP, time,
         page, user agent).
       • WhatsApp (wa.me link) — only if a visitor chooses to click through to
         chat; no data is sent unless they do.

   - NO TRACKING TODAY: There is currently NO Google Analytics, no Tag Manager,
     no Meta/Facebook pixel, no advertising cookies, and no cookie banner code
     anywhere in the site. The policy therefore states the site uses no tracking
     or advertising cookies. >>> If you later add analytics or a chat widget,
     you MUST update the Cookies and Third-Parties sections. <<<

2. LEGAL FRAMEWORK USED
   - India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) — the primary
     law. It requires: a clear notice of purpose, a lawful basis (consent or
     "legitimate use"), data-principal rights (access, correction, erasure,
     grievance redressal, nomination), and a named contact for grievances.
   - Information Technology Act, 2000 and the IT (Reasonable Security Practices
     ... Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 (SPDI Rules) — still
     relevant; require a published privacy policy and reasonable security.
   - A short international-transfer / overseas-customer note is included because
     NexoGreen serves global markets (EU/UK buyers may invoke GDPR-style rights).
     This is a light touch, not a full GDPR controller policy. If you actively
     market to EU residents and want full GDPR coverage, tell me and I'll expand
     the "Your rights" and "International transfers" sections.

3. MARKET-RESEARCH REFERENCE
   The tone and structure follow the B2B commodity-trader policies you reviewed
   (Olam, Agrocorp, Pisum, etc.): concise, business-contact focused, "we do not
   sell your data," clear retention and security statements. Those companies are
   far larger and run analytics/recruitment portals, so their policies cover
   cookies, careers data and CCTV that do NOT apply to your static brochure site.
   I deliberately did NOT copy those clauses — including data practices you don't
   actually have would be misleading and is itself a compliance risk. Copying
   another company's policy text verbatim is also not advisable (it's their
   copyrighted wording and won't match your operations).

3. THE PERSONAL DATA WE COLLECT

We collect only the information needed to respond to your business enquiry and
to provide quotations and trade documentation. This is primarily business-contact
information that you provide voluntarily.

(a) Information you give us through our forms
    • Contact form: your name, company, corporate email address, phone number,
      country, and the contents of your message.
    • Request-a-Quote (RFQ) form: your name, company, corporate email address,
      phone number, destination country/port, product or material category,
      target volume and unit, target price, packaging requirements, and any
      notes you add.

(b) Information collected automatically
    When you visit the Site, our hosting provider and certain service providers
    may automatically record standard technical information such as your IP
    address, browser type, device information, the pages you view, and the date
    and time of your visit. This is used for security, to keep the Site running,
    and to understand general usage. Our web fonts are served by Google Fonts,
    which receives your IP address in order to deliver the fonts to your browser.

We do NOT knowingly collect sensitive personal data through this Site (such as
financial account details, government identifiers, health or biometric data).
Please do not submit such information through our forms; share trade-sensitive
or financial details only through the secure channels we agree with you directly.

4. HOW AND WHY WE USE YOUR DATA (PURPOSES)

We use your personal data only for the following purposes:
    • to respond to your enquiry and to prepare and send quotations,
      specifications and trade documentation;
    • to communicate with you about your request, orders, shipments and related
      logistics;
    • to maintain our business records and to comply with applicable tax,
      customs, export–import and other legal obligations;
    • to operate, secure and improve the Site; and
    • to prevent fraud, spam and misuse (for example, our forms use an anti-spam
      check).

5. LAWFUL BASIS

We process your personal data on the basis of your consent, given when you
submit a form or contact us, and for the "legitimate uses" permitted under the
DPDP Act — namely to respond to a request you have made and to fulfil our
contractual and legal obligations. Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it
at any time (see "Your rights" below); withdrawal does not affect processing
already carried out.

6. COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

This Site does not use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or third-party
tracking or profiling technologies. We do not run advertising pixels or sell
behavioural data. Your browser may store basic technical data required for the
Site to display correctly. If we introduce analytics or other cookies in the
future, we will update this Policy and, where required, ask for your consent.

7. SHARING AND DISCLOSURE OF DATA

We do NOT sell, rent or trade your personal data, and we do not share it for
third-party marketing.

We share data only as necessary:
    • Service providers (Data Processors) acting on our instructions, including:
        - our form-handling service, [[FORM_SERVICE_NAME]], which receives and
          delivers your form submissions to us;
        - our website hosting provider; and
        - Google Fonts, which serves fonts and receives your IP address.
    • Logistics, freight, banking, inspection and customs partners, only where
      needed to process and fulfil a transaction you have requested.
    • Legal and regulatory authorities, where we are required to disclose
      information by law, regulation, court order, or to protect our rights.

If you click our WhatsApp link, you will be taken to WhatsApp and your
communication there is governed by WhatsApp's own privacy policy.

8. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

We are based in India and operate internationally. Some of our service providers
(for example, the form service and font provider) may process data on servers
located outside India. Where personal data is transferred across borders, we
take reasonable steps to ensure it remains protected and is handled in line with
this Policy and applicable law. If you are located in the EU/UK or another region
with specific data-protection rights, those rights are respected to the extent
applicable; contact us using the details below.

9. DATA RETENTION

We keep your personal data only for as long as needed for the purposes above —
typically for the duration of our business relationship and enquiry follow-up,
and thereafter for the period required to meet our legal, tax, customs and
accounting obligations. When data is no longer required, we securely delete or
anonymise it.

10. HOW WE PROTECT YOUR DATA

We apply reasonable security practices and procedures to protect personal data
against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure or destruction, in line with
the IT Act and the rules made under it. These include access controls, use of
reputable service providers, and limiting the data we collect. However, no method
of transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee
absolute security.

11. YOUR RIGHTS

Subject to applicable law, including the DPDP Act, you have the right to:
    • access a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how we process

    • request correction, completion or updating of inaccurate data;
    • request erasure of your personal data where it is no longer required;
    • withdraw any consent you have given;
    • nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of death
      or incapacity; and
    • raise a grievance about how we handle your data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Grievance Officer (below). We will
respond within the timeframes required by applicable law. You also have the right
to register a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India.

12. CHILDREN

not knowingly collect personal data of children. If you believe a child has
